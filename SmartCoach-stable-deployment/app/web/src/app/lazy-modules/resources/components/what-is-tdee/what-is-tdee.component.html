TDEE: What Is It? Why Is It So Important?

TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is the total energy used by a person during a 24-hour period. There are FOUR main components of TDEE:

◈ Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

◈ Thermic Effect of Food (TEF)

◈ Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis (NEAT)

◈ Exercise Activity Thermogenesis (EAT)

◈ Up to 75% of TDEE is BMR, which is defined as energy cost during absolute rest, such as when you sleep.
◈ Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis (NEAT) refers to the calories burned through activities other than exercise, including fidgeting, standing, and staying upright. NEAT contributes to about ~10 - 15 percent of your TDEE.
◈ The Thermic Effect of Food (TEF) is the increase in energy expenditure associated with digestion, absorption, and storage of food, and accounts for approximately 10% of the Total Daily Energy Expenditure; many believe there exist facultative as well as fixed components, yet this can be changed with the macronutrient composition of a meal.
◈ Exercise Activity Thermogenesis (EAT) or physical activity is another element of TDEE, which varies from person to person. Check out the resource 'Activity Level Guide' for more details.
◈ Weight loss occurs when you eat less than your TDEE and weight gain occurs when you eat more than your TDEE. Thus, knowing your TDEE is important for optimizing your body composition, performance, and nutrition if you have health, physique, or sports performance goals.
